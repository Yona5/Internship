Project Plan

×
The first step I took in planning the project was understanding the requirements of the project. I thoroughly read the requirements and eliminated doubts I had about them. The second step was to brainstorm website designs, get inspiration from similar projects, and identify features of the website. Having done that, I moved to making a prototype by sketching the design on paper. The third step was implementing the prototype and getting continues feedback to roll out a better version.

Background

×
After taking a break in my home country Ethiopia, I returned to Mauritius to do my internship with ALC. ALC has been working in the education sector for two years now and aims “to create a more prosperous and peaceful Africa by developing the next generation of ethical and entrepreneurial African leaders.” As a startup, ALC is a small sized company. Roughly it has 200 Employees working in the Mauritius campus. Last year, it inaugurated its Rwanda campus, expanding its horizon to provide quality education.

Job

×
During my internship, I worked as a teaching assistant. One of my responsibilities was co-facilitating programming classes. Besides co-facilitating, I identified students who need support in and outside of class . As such, I took a different approach towards those students to help them understand concepts they struggled to grasp. To support them further, I run office hours, dedicated times to have a one-on-one conversation with students. In addition, I run dojo classes which offer a platform for students to practice Java.

Reflection

×
I see the outcome of the internship in two ways, the knowledge and skills I acquired and the contribution I made. Interning with ALC for three months, I was able to get a real-world work experience and get a sense of where my career path is heading to. Coming to the contribution I made, I believe I left an impact on the students when it comes to solving programming problems.

Even though I had a good internship, there were some challenges I faced. Some I was able to solve; some I was not. And the takeaway from it was to learn from the failure and better equip myself to solve similar challenges in the future.
